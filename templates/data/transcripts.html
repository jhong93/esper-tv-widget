Searching the transcripts

For most of the videos in the data set, we have access to the transcripts. These have been time aligned at word granularity to the audio. You can search for video using transcripts with the {{ search_keys.text }}="..." predicate. For example, {{ search_keys.text }}="United Kingdom" will find all times where the phrase "United Kingdom" is spoken. In its basic usage, the {{ search_keys.text }} filter accepts individual words and phrases, where a phrase is defined as an n-gram where all the words must appear in a sequence.

This page describes some additional features of the transcript query language.

Listing words and phrases (word A or word B)

Sometimes a topic can be defined with multiple related or synonymous words/phrases. For example, the "European Union" can be also be referred to as the EU or E.U. in the transcripts. When this is the case, use the "|" character to delimit multiple words and phrases. For example, {{ search_keys.text}}="European Union | EU | E.U." will search for any of the three. This can be repeated for an arbitrary number of words and phrases.

Inflections of words

A topic can have words with many inflected forms. The simplest case is when words are singular or plural. To search for all inflected forms of a word without specifying them manually, surround the word with [...] brackets. For example, {{ search_keys.text}}="[truck]" will find instances of truck, trucks, and trucking. If multiple words are surrounded by [...], then inflections will be found for any of the words.

Windowing around mentions

By default, {{ search_keys.text}} will precisely find the intervals of time where a word or phrase is spoken. This means that each mention will count for the few hundred milliseconds when the phrase is uttered. For analyzing topic coverage, set the {{ search_keys.text_window }} to a larger value to dilate a window around each mention of the phrases. For example, {{ search_keys.text }}="immigration" AND {{ search_keys.text_window }}=30 will find video intervals starting and ending 30 seconds before and after each mention of the word immigration.
