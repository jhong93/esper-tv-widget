The Cable TV News Dataset

The dataset available for analysis using Stanford Cable TV News Analyzer was provided by the Internet Archive's TV News Archive. The dataset includes near 24-7 recordings of CNN, Fox News, and MSNBC between {{ start_date }} and {{ end_date }}. (The dataset updates daily.) In total, the dataset consists of over {{ n_total_hours }} hours of video and includes both TV news programming and commercial segments.

The Internet Archive's TV News Archive provided the dataset as a collection of video files, with each video corresponding to one airing of a news program (e.g., most videos are approximately one hour in length). Per-video metadata includes the name of the news program, the date/time it aired, and the channel on which it aired. Video frames range in resolution from 640x360 to 858x480. All videos include audio. {{ n_caption_percentage }}% of the video files have accompanying closed caption transcripts.

From this source dataset, we also compute additional labels describing the video's contents as described on the methodology page.
